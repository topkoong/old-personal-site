Park Place

Developed web app (in Software Engineering class) that serves as parking marketplace allowing owners to rent out private spaces for users to park vehicles.

  • Technologies Used: React, Nodejs, Express, MongoDB, Firebase, PayPal
  • Creator: Daniel Jonathan Kemp (Team leader), Theerut Foongkiatcharoen (Innovator), Jinwoo Song, Pratheek Basavana Gowda, Rajesh Ramesh, Rahul Vijaydev, Vibhu A Bharadwaj

What's the idea here?

Parking is inefficient all around given that in urban areas where parking demand is high but available options are scarce. Typically, drivers struggle to find a parking spot and spend an average of nearly 20 minutes in pursuit of a coveted spot. There's usually 7 a.m. street cleaning and there are hours when you cannot park your car at a given place. Sometimes, they have to wait long before someone leaves, so they can park. In a similar manner, there are problems of last dash occupancies where they spot an empty parking place, but another driver claims the spot within a short time window. However, in this day and age of digitization, a new online parking marketplace that lets owners of private parking areas rent out space to drivers can reduce a lot hassle involved in finding parking spots. It would be so much easier if the drivers could reserve a parking spot, or even “rent” one for as long as they need.

In Syracuse, driving to work or school can sometimes be a hassle because faculty and students usually arrive on four wheels. They occasionally worry about alternate side parking, meters, parking tickets and all other parking regulations. As we can see, they have trouble finding a parking space and waste a lot of time on doing so. In fact, cars are on the road for about only 5-10% of the time and take up valuable space when not in use. Therefore, we came up with the idea of an online marketplace for shared parking application that is an Airbnb-like service for a parking. We could see this working on a college campus.

For example, garage owners with some extra space in their driveways can make a few bucks because the driveways often sit idle when they are at work or out of town. The parking spot providers can post their available spot for rent. As a result, the drivers no longer need to worry about parking tickets as well as losing their prized spots to another driver. This online parking application can assist them to find and compare spots, get contact details of parking spot owner and send notifications to them when they are near the address. The parking space providers must let the drivers know of any restrictions, for example they cannot block the driveway or other paths.

The application will show the drivers where the closest or safest places (such as proximity campus safety lights) are to their destination to park. The drivers can reserve a spot in advance with their credit cards and obtain discounts for early reservations or setup recurring reservations.

All users of the app must provide contact information in the form of a username, password, valid email address, and payment information. The payment information will be in the form of a PayPal Account. Additionally, drivers must provide a description of their car (make/model/color/license plate) and parking spot providers must provide a description of their parking spot (price, location, size,photo, and restrictions).

There are plenty of parking apps out there that help you find and book a parking spot, but why an online marketplace for shared parking application is significantly different (and totally amazing) is that people could yield profit by this app. The app will help connect owners of private parking spots to drivers looking for parking, and help drivers to pay parking fee to the owners.

Additionally, it makes life easier, our cities more accessible, and people in Syracuse more connected thereby developing convenient and effective ways to find , reserve and easily pay for parking. The drivers only need to book a parking space near their destinations before leaving home. So they do not struggle to find and waste time on looking for a parking spot anymore, and it prevents them from building up parking ticket fees.

It also enables drivers to save time on the road and reduce the nation's carbon footprint because typically the drivers spend an average of nearly 20 minutes in finding a parking spot as well as losing a parking spaces to other drivers.

In conclusion, the application targets the core of the problem by offering a solution to alleviate the trouble of finding parking spaces. Through the application, owners can capitalize by renting out private spaces during times of underutilization, and drivers can seamlessly make reservations for spaces based on location and convenience. Owners can schedule spaces for durations that seem convenient for them, thus allowing them to make adjustments to when they can and cannot offer their space. Similarly, drivers can browse through a plethora of parking spaces in and around their intended destination, make recurring bookings based on space availability. The application is also flexible in a way that drivers can make another choice for a nearby location, in case an extension needs to be made and the space has been rented out for that duration.

OVERALL DESCRIPTION

This section will give an overview of the whole system. The system will be explained in its context to show how different components interact and affect each other. It will also define use cases for the application, offer a clear, yet concise explanation of the services offered with the help of system models, and different phases of client-server interaction through sequence diagrams.

  • Product Perspective

    ParkPlace is a new self-contained web application. As with all web applications, it adopts a client-server model for authentication, authorization and communication. The goal of the application is to make the process of finding parking spots easy and hassle-free. More than often, we find ourselves meandering around, desperately looking for parking areas. This can especially be seen in busy areas with limited to almost no spaces for parking. Designated public parking is one solution to the problem. However, people usually need to spend unreasonably high amounts of money for relatively short periods of parking time. This problem is addressed by ParkPlace in an easy and efficient manner.

    The client part of the application is constituted by the owner and driver. The server side of the application is modeled using ‘Firebase’ as the primary authentication and authorization server. The application logic/business logic is implemented with Express as the routing layer, built on top of the Node(Node.js) JavaScript runtime environment. The primary database is MongoDB. This is used for storing data such as user data, parking spot data, and booking and scheduling data.

    The crux of the application is a convenient and flexible booking system. The owner and driver schemas/models are interdependent in the sense that a booking made by the driver must reflect on the owner’s side of the interface. Also, map projections of parking spaces will be provided for drivers as an easy and intuitive alternative to the conventional search feature.

  • Product Functions

    The product aims to mitigate the trouble that users have to undergo on a daily basis to find parking spaces. Each of the product’s features will be elaborated in the coming sections. However, a bigger picture of some of the most important functions that the product must incorporate is essential to understanding how the product is expected to behave. Some of the important functions of the product are described below:

    • The system must allow users to have access to a clean and responsive user interface, courtesy of assimilating the best design practices.
    • It must provide a strong and robust authentication and authorization mechanism since users trust the application with personal data.
    • The system must have efficient lookup algorithms for accurate detection and mapping of parking spots.
    • The system must provide flexibility for owners to adjust ‘rent-out’ times according to their convenience.
    • Flexibility for drivers need to be ensured to allow extensions and recurring bookings for parking spots.
    • A trusted payment system must be in place to ensure hassle-free bookings.
  • User Characteristics

    The application will consist of 2 user flows, one for the owner of the parking spot, and the other for the driver. A high-level summary of the most important features offered by the system with respect to each of these users are as follows:

    • For Owner:
      • The owner assigns designated parking spaces for drivers to utilize.
      • Schedules for parking spots are flexible in a way that owners can rent out spaces only for time durations they deem as feasible or convenient for them.
      • Owners can set their own prices for spots being rented out.
      • Owners can see schedules or bookings for parking spots from drivers on a ‘View Schedule’ bulletin.
      • Owners receive notifications about bookings through emails.
      • Owners receive payments through trusted financial institutions that process transactions for them.
      • Booking extensions for drivers are subject to owner discretion. This means that the owner can choose to extend, or not extend parking durations after the booked period is over.
    • For Driver:
      • Drivers can look for parking spots in any area they wish to park.
      • Prior bookings for ‘specific’ parking spots can be made, in order to avoid missing out on the most convenient choice.
      • Recurring bookings for parking spaces can be made for different time slots.
      • Extensions for parking can be made. This is a feature that has to be agreed upon for by the owner as well.
      • Drivers can find parking spaces using graphical markers on maps, in addition to the search feature.
      • Drivers make electronic payments for bookings. This saves the extra work of needing to have the owner be present to collect money.

  • Operating Environment

    The system will implement the model-view-controller software architecture pattern. The operating environment for the system consists of a browser that renders web pages, an external web server that handles user request-response transactions, an authentication and authorization server as well as a database for storing application data. Servers with reasonable load handling capacity will be sufficient for web hosting and data storage. Central administration is vested in the web/application server.

  • Design and Implementation Constraints
    • One of the key constraints of the system is the inherent reliability on an internet connection. Since the product is implemented using the client-server model, it is imperative to have an active internet connection
    • Another important constraint is the electronic payment and booking system. Although electronic payment streamlines the payment and booking process, the system needs to support round the clock interactions with the payment gateway.
    • Administrative responsibility is relegated to the PaaS vendor. As a result, managing the application becomes a little tricky for the local administrative team.
    • Developing and handling booking cancellation policies may turn out to be tricky.
  • Assumptions and Dependencies
    • Assumptions
      • Since the system will be deployed using a cloud based hosting service, an assumption about its continuous, uninterrupted availability is made.
      • The system will depend on a trusted third party financial institution for handling monetary transactions. Hence, an assumption about total financial security is made.
    • Dependencies
      • The system will rely heavily on frameworks for user interface development, routing and data handling.
      • Also, the google maps api will be used for geocoding street addresses and marker generation for parking spots.

Interface Requirements

  • User Interfaces

    The user interface for the product will utilize a combination of HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap, and React.js, a JavaScript library for user-interface development. The user base for the application will be categorized into owners of parking spots, and drivers requiring parking spots. As a result, the user interface must support 2 different user flows, one each for the owner and the driver.

    A web application must demonstrate flexibility in a way that it must contain a fluid grid system, flexible images and media queries. As users want portable (mobile and tablet) versions of the website, it is important to follow best practices for responsive web design and development. This suggests that the website must respond to users’ behavior and environment based on screen size, platform and orientation.

    The product is expected to behave consistently across all device platforms. Each user flow is expected to see a welcome screen after visiting the website. The welcome screen generally provides information to the user about product intentions and features. In addition, each user must have access to registration and authentication screens (sign-up and login). The owner is expected to see a dashboard page that lets him/her view the number of parking spots he/she has published, a page with an embedded form for entering new parking spot details for publishing, a parking spot edit/ update page and a widget for removing a parking spot from the database. Also, a page for viewing and managing renting schedules needs to be provided.

    Coming to the drivers’ section of the interface, a search widget needs to be provided for looking for parking spots. A page showing search results consisting of parking spots in and around the searched location, and a map representation of the same needs to be designed and developed. Associated booking and confirmation web pages need to be provided.

  • Software Interfaces

    The product will use certain 3rd party libraries or API’s to supplement overall system functionality. The google maps API is used for geocoding street addresses to geographical co-ordinates that can be used to place markers on the map. These markers provide visual representation for locating parking spaces for drivers. API’s for financial transactions will be used by the product for booking spots. Communication between the core-application and the API implementation normally happens through SOAP messages.

    Software interacts with the database using JSON objects. MongoDB, the primary database vendor stores these objects in binary encoded form called BSON (or Binary JSON). The underlying operating system that runs the application server (web host) will be Linux.

  • Communications Interfaces

    The product will make use of a set of communications interface standards. They are summarized below:

    • Emails are sent to drivers when a booking has been made. Similarly, owners receive booking information through email when drivers reserve parking spots. These features depend on application layer protocols like SMTP and IMAP for their implementation.
    • Client-Server communication needs to be encrypted in order to ensure secure data transfer. Thus, we require a HTTPS/SSL (or TLS) connections between the client and server always.
    • Electronic payments for booking and confirmation must be implemented using secure, encrypted VPN tunnels. This responsibility will be relegated to the payment gateway service provider.

System Models

  • Use Case Diagram
    • For Owner

      Use case Diagram for Owner

      • Here, renter denotes the owner of the parking spot. Initially the owner registers by providing the necessary credentials or logs in using the site’s registration and authentication procedure.
      • The authentication service powered by Google’s Firebase enables the owner to register and also to successfully login to his dashboard.
      • The owner should be allowed to also publish his own space for rent on the page by providing details like photos of the parking space, the street address, contact information, duration and date for which the parking space is being rented.
      • Additionally, the owner should also be able to view the upcoming schedule of his rented parking spots along with driver details.
      • Thus, through the system, the owner is connected to the driver. The owner, while registering also provides his bank details for obtaining payment from the financial institution after the booking has been authorized.
    • For Driver

      Use case Diagram for Driver

      • A user can also be a driver looking for parking spaces near his destination. While he is able to do this without registering, in order to reserve a spot user registration is mandatory.
      • Registration and authentication processes are facilitated by Google’s Firebase authentication service.
      • Once the driver chooses the parking space of his liking, he is redirected to the booking and payment page.
      • Once there, the driver needs to make a payment through the electronic payment system, which is further verified and validated by the payment gateway service provider.
      • This completes the checkout process and the driver reserves his parking spot. If the driver is reserving a spot for the first time, it is mandatory to register.
      • The driver becomes affiliated to the owner after the reservation process.
  • Activity Diagram
    • For Owner

      Activity Diagram for Owner

      • The activity diagram for the owner is shown above.
      • The owner tries to log in and this generates an authentication request which uses Firebase to verify the identity of the user.
      • If it is an unregistered user, the user (owner or driver) can sign up.
      • After successful login, the owner is redirected to his/her dashboard. Here the owner can see thumbnails of all parking spaces published. This data is fetched from MongoDB.
      • The owner has the functionalities of publishing a new spot, editing an already published and not reserved spot, and cancelling or removing an unreserved spot from the list of published parking spots.
      • After the owner completes any one of these functionalities, he/she is redirected to the dashboard page.
      • Corresponding changes after adding, updating or removing parking spots will be reflected in the database.
    • For Driver

      Activity Diagram for Driver

      • The activity diagram for the driver explains the flow of activities for a driver who seeks to reserve a parking spot.
      • Initially any unregistered driver can view available parking spots in a certain location using the ‘search for a parking spot’ feature. A web page consisting of a list of all parking spots in and around the sought location are rendered to the driver.
      • If the driver wants to reserve the parking spot, it is mandatory to go through the registration or authentication procedure.
      • After successful registration or login, the driver is guided into his/her dashboard. Here, based on the parking spot of his liking he can initiate the checkout process.
      • The driver will first be taken to a reservation page that will present all of the parking spot details, including price, available time slots and location.
      • Once there, the driver can select the space and confirm his/her reservation.
      • This is immediately followed by the booking stage. The payment made goes through a secure gateway and is validated by the payment gateway service provider.
      • On successful reservation, the owner of the spot and driver will receive a confirmation email validating the booking process.
  • Sequence Diagram
    • Financial transactions must go through a secure, encrypted gateway (VPN tunnel). The responsibility of handling, and consequently validating payments/bookings must hence be handled by a trusted financial institution. A sequence indicating the steps for successful booking of a parking spot are listed below.
    • The driver searches for a parking spot at a location of his/her choice. Owing to a change in mind, the driver is free to go back and make another search, or opt for another parking space from the list of parking spaces displayed around the location of choice.
    • Once the driver has decided to go ahead with a parking spot, he/she reserves that spot by entering credit/ debit card details on the booking and payment page.
    • Once the payment is made, it is routed through the payment gateway, verified by the financial institution handling the payment, and then routes it to the owner’s account.
    • A booking id is generated for the driver, and a confirmation email is sent.
    • Similarly, the owner of the parking spot is notified of the booking through email.
    • The time slot corresponding to the booking is marked as ‘taken’ or ‘booked’ in the database, indicating successful reservation of the parking spot.
    • The owner and driver can now both view their reservations on their corresponding accounts in the website.
